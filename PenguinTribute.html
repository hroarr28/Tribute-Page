Penguin Tribute

Yellow-eyed penguins are the most endangered penguin species in the world, with just 4,000 left in the wild.
  1. Penguins are flightless birds that are found primarily in the Southern Hemisphere, with the exception of the Galapagos penguin, which lives near the equator.

  2. They have specialized feathers and a thick layer of blubber to keep them warm in cold water.

  3. Penguins are excellent swimmers and can reach speeds of up to 22 miles per hour in the water.

  4. There are 18 different species of penguins, each with their own unique characteristics and habitats.

  5. Emperor penguins are the tallest and heaviest of all penguin species, standing up to 120 cm tall and weighing up to 40 kg.
